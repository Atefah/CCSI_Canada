Our Mission

CCSI-Women-Canada works to promote women’s equality and empowerment in peace and security and sustainable economic growth. Our network of experienced and talented women experts in specialized areas of competence prepare and deliver training programs, implement capacity building projects, and provide leadership and mentorship to women currently working in those fields or who wish to do so. Among its goals, CCSI-W Canada aims to ensure that the application of sanctions and strategic trade control are respectful and fully compliant with human rights and international humanitarian law.

Our network of CCSI experts, including in CCSI Africa and CCSI Asia and Pacific, have conducted training programs for public and privates sector professionals of well over 50 countries assisting governments and the private sector in building their capacity in these areas: Strategic Trade Control and Sanctions policy-making, implementation enhancements, and compliance monitoring Private sector compliance policies and practices for Srategic Trade Control and Sanctions Sanctions implications for human and gender rights Monitoring and enforcement of human and gender rights and protection of humanitarian aid Preventive and protective strategies against cyber security failures, and abuses of cryptocurrencies International terrorism and counter-extremism strategies that respect gender and human rights Gender-balanced mediation and conflict reduction strategies CCSCI’s network of experts have delivered projects in partnership with numerous governmental agencies including Global Affairs Canada, the U.S. Department of State (EXBS/CTR/NDF), The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Japan, the European Union External Action Service, the African Development Bank, the Gesellschaft für Internationale Zusammenarbeit (GIZ), and companies and institutional investors.
